Journey to Freedom

The long struggle for independence through key historical moments

1857

First War of Independence

The first organized rebellion against British rule began in 1857. Muslim and Hindu soldiers united against the East India Company, marking the beginning of the freedom struggle in the subcontinent.

1906

All India Muslim League

The All India Muslim League was founded on December 30, 1906, in Dhaka. It aimed to safeguard the political rights of Muslims and later became the leading force behind Pakistan's creation.

1930

Allama Iqbal's Address

n his presidential address at the Allahabad session of the Muslim League, Allama Iqbal proposed the idea of a separate homeland for Muslims in north-western India laying the intellectual foundation for Pakistan.

1940

Pakistan Resolution

On March 23, 1940, at Minto Park (now Iqbal Park), Lahore, the Muslim League passed the historic Pakistan Resolution formally demanding independent states for Muslims, which led directly to the creation of Pakistan.

1947

Independence & Partition

On August 14, 1947, Pakistan emerged as an independent nation based on the two-nation theory. Millions migrated across the new borders, making sacrifices to shape a sovereign Muslim state.

1998

Pakistan Goes Nuclear

On May 28, 1998, Pakistan successfully conducted five nuclear tests in Chaghi, Balochistan, in response to India's nuclear tests. This act made Pakistan the 7th nuclear power in the world and the first in the Muslim world, marking a significant milestone in national defense and deterrence.

Our Heroes

The great personalities who fought for our freedom

Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah

Founder of Pakistan and its first Governor-General. Through his legal expertise and political vision, he secured a separate homeland for Muslims.

Allama Iqbal

Poet of the East and philosopher. His poetry and philosophy awakened Muslims and gave them the vision of a separate homeland.

Fatima Jinnah

Mother of the Nation and sister of Quaid-e-Azam. She worked tirelessly for women's education and political participation.

Liaquat Ali Khan

First Prime Minister of Pakistan and close companion of Quaid-e-Azam. He strengthened the foundations of the new nation.

Sir Syed Ahmad Khan

Pioneer of modern education among Muslims. He founded Aligarh University and encouraged Muslims towards modern education.

Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan

Known as the father of Pakistan's nuclear program. His scientific leadership made Pakistan a nuclear power, ensuring strong national defense and global recognition.
